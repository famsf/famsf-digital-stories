What is this “new and revolutionary art?”

Before the success, before there was even the term impressionism, Claude Monet labored to define his style and promote himself as an artist. Explore the first 14 years of Monet’s career when he struggled to provide food and shelter for himself and his family. Monet the Early Years begins with the first painting Monet exhibited in 1858 and closes in 1872, before he exhibited with the group of artists who would later be known as the Impressionists.

An Oak at Bas-Bréau (The Bodmer), 1865

Since Fontainebleau was originally a royal preserve, its trees were able to reach majestic heights seldom seen in other landscapes. One such tree that Monet painted several times was this massive oak.

Looking Closely

In this painting Monet deployed several techniques that would evolve into hallmarks of his mature style. First the artist dramatically cropped the tree, bringing the viewer up close and into the deep shadows created by the enormous branches overhead.
