Training your Eye: Composition

How an artist allocates the “real estate” of a canvas can indicate an acknowledgement of tradition or a decision to strive for innovation. With Le Phare de l’Hospice—named for the lighthouse at the center of the painting—Monet deployed the ratio of one-third sea to two-thirds sky that traces back to 17th-century Dutch and Flemish precedents such as Jan van Goyen’s Thunderstorm (1641), in the collection of the Legion of Honor.

In contrast, The Green Wave reveals Monet’s desire to disrupt viewing norms by dedicating the vast majority of the canvas to the sea. In fact, the lower half of the canvas is dedicated to nothing more than the varied tones of the water.
